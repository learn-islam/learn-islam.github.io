Question
What are your thoughts on these verses considering that Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. is a God in the Bible and in the Qur’an he is a Prophet?
Answer
Assalamu `Alaikum to my Muslim and non-Muslim readers! If you are a non-Muslim and asking the same question, welcome brother/sister! May God guide us as we try to investigate these verses. I want to begin with sincere intention all for the sake of Allah (S.W.T.)Subhanahu wa ta'ala, Arabic for "May He be glorified and exalted", Muslim honorific..

To answer this question, we need to understand why these verses are of interest to our trinitarian brothers. Specifically, we need to understand their belief about Jesus (A.S.) and his role in the doctrine of trinity.

The most popular belief, promoted by the vast majority of churches in the world, is that God has a triune nature. This is the doctrine known as the Trinity, which defines God as one Being who exists eternally as three distinct persons — the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit. Put simply, it is "one God in three persons" [1].

The trinitarians believe that Jesus (A.S.) are of the following:
  1. He was born miraculously by the virgin Marry;
  2. He was the Messiah;
  3. He is a God, specifically the Son of God of the Trinity.

Hence the reason why they pick those Qur'anic verses to support their claim, specifically the first two in the list above. So the question is,

Do we Muslims reject the miraculous birth of Jesus?
Do we recognize him as the Messiah?
Do we recognize him as God?
