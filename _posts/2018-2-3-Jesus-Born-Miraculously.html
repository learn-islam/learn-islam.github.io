Question
What are your thoughts about the miracles done by Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. considering that he is a God in the Bible and in the Qur'an he is a prophet?
Answer
Assalamu `Alaikum! Before we proceed I'd like to point out, that the above question also attempts to answer one (the miraculous birth of Jesus (A.S.)) of the questions in this post, you can check it if you are interested.

In order to understand the question, we need to agree first on the belief of our trinitarian brothers with regards to the miracles done by Jesus (A.S.).

The trinitarian believes that Jesus (A.S.) was born miraculously by the virgin Mary, and that he performed several miracles during his lifetime, and they used these among others as their proofs that Jesus (A.S.) is a God.

Their claim about the miraculous birth can be supported with the following Bible verses:
τοῦ δὲ ἰησοῦ χριστοῦ ἡ γένεσις οὕτως ἦν. μνηστευθείσης τῆς μητρὸς αὐτοῦ μαρίας τῶ ἰωσήφ, πρὶν ἢ συνελθεῖν αὐτοὺς εὑρέθη ἐν γαστρὶ ἔχουσα ἐκ πνεύματος ἁγίου.
Now the birth of Jesus Christ took place in this way. When his mother Mary had been betrothed to Joseph, before they came together she was found to be with child from the Holy Spirit.

καὶ εἶπεν ὁ ἄγγελος αὐτῇ, μὴ φοβοῦ, μαριάμ, εὖρες γὰρ χάριν παρὰ τῶ θεῶ· καὶ ἰδοὺ συλλήμψῃ ἐν γαστρὶ καὶ τέξῃ υἱόν, καὶ καλέσεις τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ ἰησοῦν.
And the angel said to her, "Do not be afraid, Mary, for you have found favor with God. And behold, you will conceive in your womb and bear a son, and you shall call his name Jesus.

These verses are just part of the detailed discussion on the birth of Jesus (A.S.), in fact among the four Gospels, the most detailed description of this miraculous birth is depicted in the book of Luke. However, these miracles did not just end at his birth, instead it propagated as he grew up where he performed several miracles to the Children of Israel, and we can find these in several places of the Gospels in the Bible. Some of these are listed in the table below.

The above list is nothing new to our Christian brothers, but what is interesting to both of us (Christians and Muslims) is what the Qur'an says about these miracles.

Do we Muslims believe in the miracles done by Jesus (A.S.)?

The answer to that is it depends. It depends on how you look at it. If you say Jesus (A.S.) did these miracles with his own powers, then we Muslims don't believe on that. However, if you say Jesus (A.S.) made these miracles through the power of the one who send him -- Allah (S.W.T.), then absolutely Yes.

In preparation for our discussion below, we need to understand as well what is the purpose of the Qur'an, since we know the fact that it was revealed after the previous scriptures (i.e. the Torah, the Gospels -- the Bible). The purpose of the revelation of the Qur'an is explained in the following verses:

شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أُنزِلَ فِيهِ ٱلْقُرْءَانُ هُدًى لِّلنَّاسِ وَبَيِّنَٰتٍ مِّنَ ٱلْهُدَىٰ وَٱلْفُرْقَانِ فَمَن شَهِدَ مِنكُمُ ٱلشَّهْرَ فَلْيَصُمْهُ وَمَن كَانَ مَرِيضًا أَوْ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ فَعِدَّةٌ مِّنْ أَيَّامٍ أُخَرَ يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ بِكُمُ ٱلْيُسْرَ وَلَا يُرِيدُ بِكُمُ ٱلْعُسْرَ وَلِتُكْمِلُوا۟ ٱلْعِدَّةَ وَلِتُكَبِّرُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ مَا هَدَىٰكُمْ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ
The month of Ramadhan [is that] in which was revealed the Qur'an, a guidance for the people and clear proofs of guidance and criterion. So whoever sights [the new moon of] the month, let him fast it; and whoever is ill or on a journey - then an equal number of other days. Allah intends for you ease and does not intend for you hardship and [wants] for you to complete the period and to glorify Allah for that [to] which He has guided you; and perhaps you will be grateful.

According to Allah (S.W.T.)Subhanahu wa ta'ala, Arabic for "May He be glorified and exalted", Muslim honorific., the Qur'an was revealed as a criterion over the previous scriptures. That is, we must use the Qur'an to verify if such revelation is true or false. For example, according to the new testament of the Christian bible, it says that God has taken a son, but the Qur'an rejects this.
قَالُوا۟ ٱتَّخَذَ ٱللَّهُ وَلَدًا سُبْحَٰنَهُۥ هُوَ ٱلْغَنِىُّ لَهُۥ مَا فِى ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ إِنْ عِندَكُم مِّن سُلْطَٰنٍۭ بِهَٰذَآ أَتَقُولُونَ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ مَا لَا تَعْلَمُونَ
They have said, "Allah has taken a son." Exalted is He; He is the [one] Free of need. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is in the earth. You have no authority for this [claim]. Do you say about Allah that which you do not know?

Notice how the verse commenced, "They have said ...". This phrase simply proves how Qur'an looks at the previous scriptures, it tries to confirm and correct the mistakes done, because obviously we were not alive in that time, and as the verse states "... Do you say about Allah that which you do not know?". This is also to counter the argument made by the trinitarians, that if the previous scripture is wrong and the Qur'an confirms those scriptures, then the Qur'an is also wrong.
