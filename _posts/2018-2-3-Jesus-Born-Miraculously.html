Question
What are your thoughts on these considering that Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. is a God in the Bible and in the Qur'an he is a prophet?
Answer
Assalamu `Alaikum! In order to understand the question, we first need to agree on the belief of our trinitarian brothers with regards to the miracles done by Jesus (A.S.).

The most popular belief, promoted by the vast majority of churches in the world, is that God has a triune nature. This is the doctrine known as the Trinity, which defines God as one Being who exists eternally as three distinct persons — the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit. Put simply, it is "one God in three persons" [1].

The trinitarians further believe that Jesus (A.S.) was born miraculously by the virgin Mary, and that he performed several miracles during his lifetime and is the Messiah, and they used these among others as their proofs that Jesus (A.S.) is a God.

The claim about the miraculous birth can be supported with the following Bible verses:
τοῦ δὲ ἰησοῦ χριστοῦ ἡ γένεσις οὕτως ἦν. μνηστευθείσης τῆς μητρὸς αὐτοῦ μαρίας τῶ ἰωσήφ, πρὶν ἢ συνελθεῖν αὐτοὺς εὑρέθη ἐν γαστρὶ ἔχουσα ἐκ πνεύματος ἁγίου.
Now the birth of Jesus Christ took place in this way. When his mother Mary had been betrothed to Joseph, before they came together she was found to be with child from the Holy Spirit.
Bible (ESV)
Matthew
1:18
καὶ εἶπεν ὁ ἄγγελος αὐτῇ, μὴ φοβοῦ, μαριάμ, εὖρες γὰρ χάριν παρὰ τῶ θεῶ· καὶ ἰδοὺ συλλήμψῃ ἐν γαστρὶ καὶ τέξῃ υἱόν, καὶ καλέσεις τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ ἰησοῦν.
And the angel said to her, "Do not be afraid, Mary, for you have found favor with God. And behold, you will conceive in your womb and bear a son, and you shall call his name Jesus.
Bible (ESV)
Luke
1:30-31

These verses are just part of an extensive discussion of the birth of Jesus (A.S.), in fact among the four Gospels, the most detailed description of this miraculous birth is depicted in the book of Luke. However, these miracles did not just end at his birth, instead it propagated as he grew up where he performed several miracles to the Children of Israel, and we can find these in several places in the Gospels in the Bible. Some of these are listed in the table below.
Miracles References
Incarnation of Jesus Matthew 1:18-25, Luke 1:26-80
Heals the Demoniac Mark 1:23-26, Luke 4:33-36
Cleanses the Leper Matthew 9:1-8, Mark 2:1-12, Luke 5:17-26
Restoring the Withered Hand Matthew 12:9-13, Mark 3:1-5, Luke 6:6-11
Raises Jairus' Daughter Matthew 9:18-19, 9:23-26; Mark 22-24, 35-43; etc.
Stills the Storm Matthew 8:28-34, Matthew 14:32, Mark 4:35-41, Luke 8:22-25

The above list is nothing new to our Christian brothers, but what is interesting to both of us (Christians and Muslims) is what the Qur'an says about these miracles.

Do we Muslims believe in the miracles done by Jesus (A.S.)?

The answer to that is, it depends. It depends on how you look at it. If you say Jesus (A.S.) did these miracles with his own powers, then we Muslims don't believe on that. However, if you say Jesus (A.S.) made these miracles through the power of the one who sent him -- Allah (S.W.T.), then Yes, we absolutely agree on that.

I will let the Qur'an speak for itself and answer the question, and then I will give my thoughts before I end this post. For purpose of readability, we'll keep the verses into sections:
