Question
What are your thoughts on these considering that Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. is a God in the Bible and in the Qur'an he is a prophet?
Answer
Assalamu 'Alaikum!Arabic for "Peace be upon you", Muslim greeting. In order to understand the question, we first need to agree on the belief of our trinitarian brothers with regards to the miracles done by Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. .

The most popular belief, promoted by the vast majority of churches in the world, is that God has a triune nature. This is the doctrine known as the Trinity, which defines God as one Being who exists eternally as three distinct persons — the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit. Put simply, it is "one God in three persons" [1].

The trinitarians further believe that Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. was born miraculously by the virgin Mary, and that he performed several miracles during his lifetime and is the Messiah, and they used these among others as their proofs that Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. is a God.

The claim about the miraculous birth can be supported with the following Bible verses:
τοῦ δὲ ἰησοῦ χριστοῦ ἡ γένεσις οὕτως ἦν. μνηστευθείσης τῆς μητρὸς αὐτοῦ μαρίας τῶ ἰωσήφ, πρὶν ἢ συνελθεῖν αὐτοὺς εὑρέθη ἐν γαστρὶ ἔχουσα ἐκ πνεύματος ἁγίου.
Now the birth of Jesus the Messiah took place in this way. When his mother Mary had been engaged to Joseph, but before they lived together, she was found to be with child from the Holy Spirit.

These verses are just part of an extensive discussion of the birth of Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific., in fact among the four Gospels, the most detailed description of this miraculous birth is depicted in the book of Luke. However, these miracles did not just end at his birth, instead it propagated as he grew up where he performed several miracles to the Children of Israel, and we can find these in several places in the Gospels in the Bible. Some of these are listed in the table below.

The above list is nothing new to our Christian brothers, but what is interesting to both of us (Christians and Muslims) is what the Qur'an says about these miracles.

Do we Muslims believe in the miracles done by Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific.?

The answer to that is, it depends. It depends on how you look at it. If you say Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. did these miracles with his own powers, then we Muslims don't believe on that. However, if you say Jesus (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. did these miracles just like how Moses (A.S.)ʿAlayhi as-salām, Arabic for "Peace be upon him", Muslim honorific. and other prophets did their miracles, that is, not with their own power but through the power of the one who sent them -- Allah (S.W.T.)Subhanahu wa ta'ala, Arabic for "May He be glorified and exalted", Muslim honorific., then Yes, we absolutely agree on that.

I will let the Qur'an speak for itself and answer the question, and then I will give my thoughts before I end this post. For purpose of readability, we'll keep the verses into sections:
