--- layout: post title: "AC: Allah brings about both Good and Evil" date: 2018-08-26 1:00:00 +0800 categories: Answering Christianity Qur'an Al-Baqara An-Nahl Yunus 2:106 16:101 comments: true --- Whereas the God of the Qur'an brings about both good and evil, the God of the Bible never engages in evil and is singularly righteous (1 John 1:5)
Question
Is the above statement true?
Answer
Assalamu 'Alaikum!Arabic for "Peace be upon you", Muslim greeting. Alhamdulillah!Arabic for "Praise be to God", Muslim common phrase.

If we are to interpret the above statement, it seems to suggest that the God of the Qur'an is not righteous since it also brings evil to human beings. If that's what you believed, then let's investigate the Qur'an if such statement is true.

God by definition should be loving and caring, and should never bring forth evil to His creation. However, we need to understand that God created Satan as well. Anything evil is of course from Satan and not from God. Further, if God indeed loves us, then He should clearly warned us of Satan.

In Islam, everything around us is already well-planned and well-designed according to the will of Allah (S.W.T.), from the very start of creation. So any decision we do is NOT A COINCIDENCE, Allah (S.W.T.) knows ahead of time what our actions would be. However, in Islam, despite the decision we made somehow are already programmed, we are also told that, we have our OWN FREE WILL in our decision making. This seems to be difficult to comprehend at first glance, since NOT A COINCIDENCE and WITH FREE WILL are better interpreted as mutually exclusive. But, that is indeed possible, in which our FREE WILLED decisions end up to what God actually planned. To have the two (NOT A COINCIDENCE and WITH FREE WILL) to work well together, is one reason what makes Allah (S.W.T.) indeed the one and only true God, because those attributes are difficult to maintain or even comprehend.

Having the above understanding, it suffices to say that Allah brings forth goodness to us and not evil, since it is up to us whether to follow Allah or to follow Satan. Whatever is our decision, is our own free will. If we follow Allah, then goodness is indeed from Him. However, if we follow Satan, then there is no any goodness in it, and the absence of goodness is evil.

While we believe evil is from Satan, we also need to account that God created him in the first place. Therefore technically, everything we received is indeed from the will of Allah even those from the works of Satan, but as a test. This follows from the fact that God by definition is the creator of everything, including Satan. Caution, don't rush and conclude that Allah brings forth evil through Satan, because in the end, Satan has his own free will, his decision is his own and not influenced by Allah (S.W.T.) (though Allah (S.W.T.) knows already the actions of Satan ahead of time).

I think the above discussion is enough to conclude that such statement, that Allah brings forth evil, is not true. What left for me then, is to dump the verses as proof to the above logic.
