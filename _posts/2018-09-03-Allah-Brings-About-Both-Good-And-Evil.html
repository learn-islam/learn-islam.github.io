--- layout: post title: "AC: Allah brings about both Good and Evil" date: 2018-08-26 1:00:00 +0800 categories: Answering Christianity Qur'an Al-Baqara An-Nahl Yunus 2:106 16:101 comments: true --- Whereas the God of the Qur'an brings about both good and evil, the God of the Bible never engages in evil and is singularly righteous (1 John 1:5)
Question
Is the above statement true?
Answer
Assalamu 'Alaikum!Arabic for "Peace be upon you", Muslim greeting. Alhamdulillah!Arabic for "Praise be to God", Muslim common phrase.

If we are to interpret the above statement, it seems to suggest that the God of the Qur'an is not righteous since it also brings evil to human beings. If that's what you believed, then let's investigate the Qur'an if such statement is true.

God by definition should be loving and caring, and should never bring forth evil to His creation. However, we need to understand that God created Satan as well. Anything evil is of course from Satan and not from God. Further, if God indeed loves us, then He should clearly warned us of Satan and give us reason why He created Satan in the first place.

In Islam, everything around us is well-planned and well-designed according to the will of Allah (S.W.T.), from the very start of creation. So any decision we do is NOT A COINCIDENCE, Allah (S.W.T.) knows ahead of time what our actions would be. However, in Islam, despite the decisions we made somehow are already programmed, we are also told that, we have our OWN FREE WILL in our decision making. At first glance, there seems to be conflict since NOT A COINCIDENCE and WITH FREE WILL are better interpreted as mutually exclusive. But, that is indeed possible, in which our FREE WILLED decisions end up to what God actually planned. To have the two (NOT A COINCIDENCE and WITH FREE WILL) to work well together, is one reason why Allah (S.W.T.) is the one and only true God, because those attributes are difficult to maintain or even easy to comprehend.

Having the above understanding, it suffices to say that Allah (S.W.T.) brings forth goodness to us and not evil, since it is up to us whether to follow Allah (S.W.T.) or to follow Satan. Whatever our decisions may be, it is, by design, our own free will. If we follow Allah (S.W.T.), then goodness is indeed from Him. However, if we follow Satan, then there is no goodness in it, and the absence of goodness is evil.

While we believe evil is from Satan, we also argued above that God created him in the first place. Therefore technically, everything we received is indeed from the will of Allah (S.W.T.) even those from the works of Satan. But what is the reason behind it? How can a loving and caring God create evil for us human beings? In Islam, Allah (S.W.T.) created evil as a test. Otherwise, if there is no test (such as presence of evil), then what is the point of having heaven and hell? Indeed, Allah (S.W.T.) created the heaven and hell for those who deserve it. In order to determine who qualified for heaven or hell, there should be a way to segment human beings into groups belonging to heaven or hell. So how does universities choose their students? What do they do to determine those deserving? They tests us right? Remember those entrance tests? That's how they do it. Therefore, applying the same analogy, Allah (S.W.T.) tests us as well in order for him to tag (though Allah (S.W.T.) knows this already) those who follow Satan and those who follow Him. Unlike the approaches done by the universities, Allah (S.W.T.), the Most Merciful, already told us the answer to all tests, and that is, to trust Him and follow Him only, and not Satan who is our clear enemy.

Moreover, in Islam, goodness is not always a blessing for those having weak faith. Sometimes, Allah (S.W.T.) tests us with goodness by giving us power and wealth. If you have weak faith, you will likely fail this test, since there are lots of temptations (evil things like night clubs, drugs, etc.) one would likely fall into. Satan constantly whispers and do suggestions that even goodness can turn into evil. Hence, good can be evil in disguise, and evil can be blessing in disguise (say poverty which you might consider as evil, is actually a blessing in disguise because you are constrained to what you have, and not be able to go for bar/drugs/etc. that rich guys are sometimes tempted to do). So don't rush and conclude that Allah brings forth evil through Satan, because in the end, Satan has his own free will, his decision is his own and not influenced by Allah (S.W.T.) (though Allah (S.W.T.) knows already the actions of Satan ahead of time).

The above discussion is enough to conclude that such statement, that Allah brings forth evil, is not true. Feel free to counter my argument if you disagree. What left for me then, is to dump the verses as proofs to the above logic.
